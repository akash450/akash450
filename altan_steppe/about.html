About Us

Our resort was founded in 1978 by the late Bilguun Gaanbaatar. He arrived to Australia as a young working-class boy. He learnt from his parents that their family was descended from the legendary warrior and leader of the largest Mongol empire, Chinggis Khaan. He was fascinated with this idea and decided to research the raw and nomadic way of life in 13th century Mongolia. Thus, the 'Altan Steppe' was born initially as 'The Gaanbaatar Museum of Chinggis Khaan'. Slowly, upon advice from close relatives, and upon noticing the local popularity of the museum, he converted his museum into a small campsite, showcasing his childhood passion through fun, memorable activities that immerses the guest in the way of life of the ancient Mongols. Eventually, we evolved into the nationally awarded and critically acclaimed resort we are known as today. After the passing of Mr Gaanbaatar in 2015, his son Naargi continued to uphold his father's legacy through the introduction of the 'games arena' which created a boost in employment and added to the simple, wholesome experience felt by guests from all across the world.
